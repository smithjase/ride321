The Challenge

The hardest problems
demand the hardest effort

A two-day road cycling circumnavigation of Mont Blanc — Western Europe's highest peak — crossing through France, Italy and Switzerland. Over 320 kilometres and approximately 8,000 metres of climbing through some of the most dramatic alpine scenery on earth. This isn't a sportive. It's a mission.

The Route

Around the monarch
of the Alps

Starting and finishing in Chamonix, the route loops around the entire Mont Blanc massif on some of the most legendary cycling roads in Europe — cols that have forged legends in the Tour de France, the Giro d'Italia and beyond.

Every pedal stroke takes you deeper into the heart of the Alps: soaring switchbacks, glacial valleys, alpine meadows, and three distinct cultures — each with their own food, language, and way of life.

Day 01
Chamonix → Courmayeur → Aosta
From France into the Italian Alps. Cross the high passes, descend into the Val d'Aosta, and feel the temperature — and the espresso — change.
~160km Distance
~4,000m Climbing
Day 02
Aosta → Martigny → Chamonix
Through the Great St. Bernard Pass into Switzerland, along the Rhône Valley, and back over into France. The final kilometres into Chamonix will stay with you forever.

The Cause

Riding to outsmart cancer

AI is transforming how we detect, diagnose and treat cancer. Every kilometre we ride funds the technology and training that will save lives.

All funds raised go directly to The London Clinic Academy's AI Fund — supporting the development and adoption of artificial intelligence in cancer care. From early detection algorithms to treatment planning tools, AI is accelerating breakthroughs that give patients better outcomes, faster.

The hardest climbs on the road mirror the hardest fights in the clinic. This ride connects human endurance with human ingenuity — because beating cancer takes both.
